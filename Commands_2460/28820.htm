Guarding

Guarding is an effective way to reduce the leakage current and capacitance that can exist between HI and LO. A guard is a low impedance point in the circuit that is at nearly the same potential as the high impedance lead that is being guarded. Use guarding when you are sourcing or measuring low current (less than 1 μA) or when test circuit impedance is more than 1 GW. Also use guard in noisy environments.

The rear panel of the Model 2460 includes a current‑limited (100 µA minimum) driven cable guard designed to drive cable capacitance at the sense HI and force HI connections. This guard is always enabled and provides a buffered voltage. For 2‑wire measurements, guard is at the same level as the force HI voltage. For 4‑wire measurements, it is at the same level as the sense HI voltage.

To use the built‑in guards of the Model 2460, you must use the rear‑panel screw terminal connection. There are no guards available on the front panel.


Guard is at the same potential as output HI. Therefore, if hazardous voltages are present at output HI, they are also present at the GUARD terminal. Failure to heed this warning may result in personal injury or death due to electric shock.
