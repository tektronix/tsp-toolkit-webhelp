Internal reference junction

When the internal reference junction is selected, the DAQ6510 measures the temperature of the cold junction (screw terminals), measures the input voltage, and then calculates the temperature reading at the thermocouple. For specific switching modules, the cold junction can be the switching module's screw terminals with voltage temperature sensors strategically placed to measure the temperature of the cold junction.

To help maintain stability and accuracy over time and changes in temperature, the DAQ6510 periodically measures internal voltages corresponding to offsets (zero) and amplifier gains. For thermocouple temperature measurements using the internal reference junction, the internal temperature is also measured. These measurements are used in the algorithm to calculate the reading of the input signal. This process is known as autozeroing.

The internal reference junction is only valid on the rear inputs for cards that support CJC, such as the 7700, 7706, 7708, and 7710 switching modules.


Internal temperature references are collected whether or not autozero is enabled.
